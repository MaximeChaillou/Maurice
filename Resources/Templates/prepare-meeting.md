# Meeting Preparation

## Instructions

1. **Identify the meeting folder** from `$0` (e.g. `Meetings/Team-Sync/`)
2. **Gather context:**
   - Read the last 3 meeting notes in this folder
   - Identify participants from previous notes
   - Read `Memory/Directory.md` to identify participants and their context
   - For each participant with a file in `Memory/People/`, read it — note recurring topics, patterns, and current focus areas
   - For participants with `managed: true`, also check their latest 1:1 and objectives in `People/`
   - Read project files in `Memory/Projects/` linked to participants (check `projects` field in their people file)
   - Read `Memory/Company.md` if the meeting touches organization or strategy topics
   - Identify ongoing actions and open topics from previous meetings
   - Read `next.md` in the meeting folder and integrate pending topics
   - Search Slack for recent messages related to the meeting topic or participants
   - Search Notion for relevant pages or databases linked to the meeting topics
3. **Analyze:**
   - Identify follow-up items still in progress
   - Spot recurring themes or unresolved issues across meeting notes AND people files
   - Ignore topics already completed
4. **Create the preparation note:**
   - Create `Meetings/<folder>/YYYY-MM-DD.md` (today's date)
   - Use a lightweight, scannable format:
     - Frontmatter (type, date, participants)
     - Topics to discuss / questions to ask — simple checklist (`- [ ]`)
     - Open actions from previous meetings

## Rules
- Keep it **short and actionable** — no empty sections, no heavy templates
- Language: French
- Focus on what needs to be discussed, not a recap of the past
